hound_client::call

Module policy 

Source
Expand description

Pure, unit-tested call policy — the sans-IO decision layer. Inputs (sizes, instants, samples, roster caps) in, decisions out: codec negotiation + the mid-call CodecSwitch, spatial-layer LayerHysteresis, RestartPacer, ResizeWatch, mic route warmup / dead-silence detection, per-stream playback gain, and the audio gap/cadence math. Nothing here touches sockets, clocks, or devices, so every policy is pinned by unit tests without a live source.

Structs§

CadenceDiag 🔒
Per-stream receive-cadence diagnostic (a retained debugging tool). Accumulates over a window and, on completion, reports the signals that explain an active-stream gap the smoothed jitter number hides — see the call site. Pure bookkeeping; no IO.
CodecSwitch 🔒
Decides when a live encoder should switch codecs as the roster changes. Pure (no IO) so the policy is unit-tested. Downgrades land immediately — a peer that can’t decode the current codec has no video until we drop to one it can — while upgrades are debounced (a peer leaving lets us climb, but there’s no rush, and rushing invites thrash if they rejoin).
LayerHysteresis 🔒
Per-(peer, source) hysteresis for the spatial-layer subscription decision. Pure (no IO) so the policy is unit-tested in isolation. Self::observe takes the raw per-tile demand and returns the committed demand the subscribe loop should act on: a change that would switch the subscribed track (source ⇄ low) commits only once the new layer has held for debounce AND the previous switch is at least dwell old; a change that does NOT switch tracks (a different cap value while already on the low layer, or no change) passes through immediately. This is what makes a rejoin’s relayout flap — or a slow focus-toggle alternation — a bounded trickle instead of a resubscribe storm on the shared connection.
MicRouteWarmup 🔒
One-shot mic route warmup: a non-48 kHz capture device (a Bluetooth headset in HFP mode) may open onto an SCO route that is not carrying audio yet — the stream runs, delivering silence, and no error ever fires. Reopening once, [hound_engine::api::ROUTE_WARMUP_DELAY] after open, lands on the settled route; 48 kHz devices never arm. The playout side has the same policy inside the mix bus; this is the capture-side half.
MicSilenceWatch 🔒
Detects a dead-silent capture stream (see MIC_SILENCE_WARN_FRAMES). Self::observe returns true exactly once per silent streak, at the threshold; any non-zero sample resets the streak (and re-arms the warning).
PliThrottle 🔒
Rate-limits a pipeline’s recovery keyframe requests (RECOVERY_PLI_MIN_INTERVAL). Pure; unit-tested.
ResizeWatch 🔒
The resize watch’s running state inside the capture loop, factored out PURE so the debounce classification is unit-testable without a live capture source. Tracks the baseline content size and a pending (changed-but-not-yet-held) candidate.
RestartPacer 🔒
Pure restart-pacing policy for reaped pipelines (unit-tested). A HEALTHY death — the pipeline delivered frames and lived ≥ RESTART_PACE — restarts immediately and resets the track’s ladder (a live stream hit a transient fault; recover fast). An UNHEALTHY death (instant kill, or a subscription that never delivered anything) climbs an exponential ladder, so a track the relay/producer keeps wedging retries forever at a decaying, bounded rate instead of churning the shared connection. Self::ready_at is what the reconcile consults.
StreamVolumes 🔒
Per-remote-audio-stream playback gain, keyed (peer, source) — the tile right-click volume/mute control. Each audio pipeline holds its stream’s gain CELL (an AtomicU32 of f32 bits, loaded lock-free per frame); the map only locks on the ~UI-rate Self::set / pipeline-start Self::cell paths. Lives on ConnectionParams so a volume survives reconnects and resubscribes (the cell is looked up by key, not by pipeline instance).

Constants§

CADENCE_WINDOW_FRAMES 🔒
How many decoded frames per cadence-diagnostic window (~5 s at 50 fps).
CODEC_UPGRADE_DEBOUNCE 🔒
How long a richer codec must stay negotiable before we actually switch UP to it, so a peer flapping in/out of the call doesn’t thrash the encoder.
LAYER_SWITCH_DEBOUNCE 🔒
How long a tile’s demanded spatial layer must HOLD before we switch its video subscription between the source and low tracks. A switch tears down + reopens a moq subscription on the connection shared with audio; doing it on every transient tile resize (the Flutter view relays out after a (re)join, firing a burst of sizes that flap source→low→source within a second) churns the connection’s stream state and disrupts AUDIO delivery (an “audio is sacred” violation). Holding the demand until it is durable collapses that burst to at most one switch. ~1.5 s is below human patience for a focus-to-sharpen yet well above any relayout flap.
LAYER_SWITCH_DWELL 🔒
Minimum dwell on a layer after an actuated switch before the NEXT switch may commit. The debounce alone only filters flaps shorter than itself; a demand that alternates slower than the debounce (a stage↔filmstrip focus toggle re-laying tiles out every couple of seconds) would pass it and thrash the subscription anyway. The dwell bounds actuated churn to one switch per window per stream regardless of the flap period; a held demand still actuates the moment the dwell expires.
MAX_PLC_FRAMES 🔒
Max consecutive frames concealed with Opus PLC on a timestamp gap. A gap of up to this many frames (~100 ms at 20 ms/frame) is plausibly recoverable brief loss worth concealing; beyond that the gap is the sender being silent (DTX emits no frames) or a capture discontinuity — fabricating extrapolated audio there is wrong (it mislabels silence as loss and splices garbage into an active stream). Silence is played across the larger gap instead.
MIC_SILENCE_WARN_FRAMES 🔒
Consecutive exactly-zero mic frames before warning once that the input is delivering pure silence — a dead capture route (BT/SCO, revoked permission, a silent virtual device) produces bit-exact zeros, which a live room’s noise floor never does. ~3 s at 20 ms frames.
RECOVERY_PLI_MIN_INTERVAL 🔒
Minimum spacing between the recovery keyframe requests a video pipeline sends outside the subscribe-time nag — on a mid-stream stall or after a backpressure drop broke decode continuity. The producer coalesces duplicates through its own throttle; this just keeps a long producer pause from turning into a control-plane drip.
RESTART_PACE 🔒
Base restart pace for a reaped pipeline, and the first rung of the escalation ladder: an UNHEALTHY death (died fast, or never delivered a frame) waits this long, doubling per consecutive unhealthy death up to RESTART_PACE_MAX.
RESTART_PACE_MAX 🔒
Ladder ceiling: a persistently-dead track (producer gone dark, relay routing wedged) keeps retrying forever — resubscribes are how it eventually heals — but never faster than this once the ladder tops out.
SOURCE_LIVENESS_IDLE 🔒
How long the screen capture must be IDLE (no real frames delivered) before the loop starts probing whether the source still exists. Static content is legitimately idle, so probing starts only after a quiet spell.
SOURCE_LIVENESS_INTERVAL 🔒
Minimum spacing between source-liveness probes (each is a blocking ~tens-of-ms shareable-content fetch on the otherwise-idle capture thread).

Functions§

apply_gain 🔒
Scale decoded PCM by gain, saturating at the i16 rails (a >1 boost must clip gracefully, never wrap). Unity is the caller’s fast path — call only when gain != 1.0. Pure; unit-tested.
codec_bitrate_factor 🔒
Bitrate scale per codec for EQUAL perceptual quality, relative to H.264. A more efficient codec reaches the same quality at fewer bits, so we bank the efficiency as lower bandwidth — and the per-packet receive CPU that dominates a screenshare — instead of a sharper picture at the same bitrate. The BWE rung stays the quality target (an H.264-equivalent ceiling); this scales what the encoder actually emits. Conservative vs the codecs’ headline gains (HEVC ~40-50%, AV1 ~50%+, more still on screen content) so quality never regresses.
codec_from_rank 🔒
codec_rank 🔒
Encode a [VideoCodec] for the AtomicU8 codec slot shared with the encode loop. The value doubles as a priority rank (higher = richer), so the re-negotiator can tell a downgrade from an upgrade with a plain comparison.
frames_missing 🔒
Frames genuinely missing in a gap_us jump between consecutive in-order frame timestamps (frame size frame_us). Tolerant of sub-frame capture-timing jitter: SCO/resampled sources never space frames at exactly 20 ms, so a gap under ~1.5 frames is the next frame arriving a little late, NOT a loss. Counting it would fabricate one spurious PLC frame per real frame (concealment dwarfing real audio). Pure + unit-tested. The caller separately caps the result at MAX_PLC_FRAMES.
low_screen_dims 🔒
The LOW simulcast layer’s encode geometry, derived from the SOURCE so it preserves aspect ratio — a fixed 16:9 rung (e.g. 640×360) would stretch any non-16:9 share (a near-square window gets squished horizontally). Scale the source so its long edge is LOW_SCREEN_LONG_EDGE, even-clamped (the encoder needs even extents): a 16:9 source still yields 640×360, a 1052×1080 window yields ~624×640, a portrait phone-mirror its own tall geometry — all at the source aspect, so scaled_to (which stretches to the exact target) downscales without distortion.
negotiate_codec 🔒
Pick the video codec to PUBLISH: the highest rung on the encode ladder (AV1 → H.265 → H.264, [hound_engine::types::ENCODE_PRIORITY]) this device can hardware-encode AND every current remote peer can decode (so the forward-no-transcode relay never hands a peer a codec it can’t play), falling back to H.264. Read from the live roster: a less-capable peer shrinks common_remote_decode_caps, an all-modern call gets HEVC/AV1. encode_caps is probed once by the caller (the probe spins a throwaway VT session) and reused — at publish time and by the mid-call re-negotiator on every roster change.
source_probe_due 🔒
Whether an idle capture loop should probe source liveness now. Pure — the throttle policy in one testable place: probe only after a real-frame drought of SOURCE_LIVENESS_IDLE, at most every SOURCE_LIVENESS_INTERVAL.
video_codec_to_ctl 🔒
Map an engine [VideoCodec] onto the control-plane CtlCodec announced in Publish. Advisory — the per-packet wire byte is the decode source of truth.
wants_low_layer 🔒
Whether a quality request selects the low spatial layer (a capped tile) vs the source track. The track-switch axis the hysteresis debounces.
wire_codec 🔒
Map the control-plane codec onto the wire codec byte. The two enums are kept in sync deliberately (see hound_control::Codec docs). Used by the publish path’s codec-negotiation seam (and pinned by a unit test); kept here as the single source of truth even though the camera codec is currently hardcoded to H.264.
