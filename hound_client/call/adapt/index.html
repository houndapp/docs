Module adapt 

Source
Expand description

Sender-side BWE/congestion adaptation. The camera + screen video the client PUBLISHES adapts its bitrate/resolution to network conditions and serves keyframes on demand. The brain — [hound_media::VideoAdaptation] — is shared; this is the local IO glue:

  • MediaFeedback — the in-process form of ServerMessage::MediaFeedbackReceived fanned out of the control reader to the per-track feedback listeners.
  • AdaptShared — the atomics seam between the async 1 s adaptation TICK (owns the VideoAdaptation core, reads conn.rtt()) and the !Send capture/encode thread (owns the encoder, applies the decision per frame). One writer per field.
  • apply_adaptation_tick — the pure glue: drain backpressure + a pending PLI + the worst receiver-reported loss, advance the core, write the decision back.
  • spawn_keyframe_listener / spawn_feedback_listener — the producer side of the PLI relay + the receiver→producer BWE loop, each FILTERED to OUR track of a given Source (Camera vs Screen) via the SessionView.

With good RTT + no loss the ladder sits at the top rung, so a healthy network is byte-identical to the pre-adaptation publish path.

Structs§

AbortOnDrop 🔒
Owns a [JoinHandle] and aborts the task on drop. Ties an adaptation listener’s lifetime to its publish task: when the publish task returns (or is aborted on leave/reconnect teardown), the listener is aborted too.
AdaptShared 🔒
The shared seam between the async adaptation tick (owns the sans-IO [VideoAdaptation] core + observes RTT) and the !Send capture/encode thread (owns the encoder, applies the decision per frame). Plain atomics — no lock crosses the thread/async boundary and each field has exactly one writer.
MediaFeedback 🔒
One receiver reception report relayed to us (the producer) for one of our published tracks — the in-process form of [ServerMessage::MediaFeedbackReceived]. The per-track feedback listener folds the loss fraction into its sender-side BWE (serve-the-worst-leg across reporters). Counts are deltas since the reporter’s previous report.
QualityReport 🔒
A receiver’s standing quality demand for one of OUR published video tracks — the in-process form of [ServerMessage::QualityRequested]. The per-track quality listener feeds it into a [hound_media::SimulcastPlanner] (per-receiver cap map); the resulting LayerPlan decides whether to spin up the shared low layer. A Capped cap below source ⇒ that receiver wants the low track.

Functions§

apply_adaptation_tick 🔒
The pure glue between AdaptShared (the atomics the capture thread reads/writes) and the sans-IO [VideoAdaptation] core, with RTT passed in. Drains the backpressure counters + a pending PLI + the worst receiver-reported loss, advances the core, and writes the decision back (target bitrate/fps/geometry, paused, forced keyframe). No IO here — unit-testable by driving the atomics directly.
drive_video_publish 🔒
Drive one video publish loop: the 1 s adaptation tick + frame forwarding, with the live gate deciding when forwarding starts. The tick runs from the first iteration — before any subscriber — so PLIs are serviced and the BWE keeps state even while dark; only forwarding waits for proof of a receiver.
encode_and_publish_adaptive 🔒
The shared per-frame encode→adapt→publish core for both video capture loops. Retargets the encoder to the BWE’s latest bitrate (only on a real move), honours a throttle-approved keyframe (swap-clears so it fires once), downsamples to the rung’s fps, downscales to the rung geometry when smaller than capture, encodes, and forwards each access unit via forward. forward returns false on backpressure (its bounded channel was full); the caller’s drop bumps dropped, which the adaptation tick reads as the congestion signal. A forced keyframe is re-armed when it is dropped, so the next captured frame re-emits it.
open_encoder_laddered 🔒
Open an encoder at target, falling back DOWN the ladder (hound_engine::types::ENCODE_PRIORITY: AV1 → H.265 → H.264) to the first codec that opens. Safe to descend without re-checking decode caps: the negotiated target means every peer decodes it, and real decode hardware is monotonic (AV1-capable ⇒ H.265-capable ⇒ H.264), so every lower rung is decodable too. H.264 is the always-buildable floor, so this returns None only if even H.264 won’t open.
run_adaptation_tick 🔒
Read the connection RTT (the one bit of IO) and advance the adaptation one tick. Splitting the RTT read out keeps apply_adaptation_tick pure + unit-testable.
spawn_codec_renegotiator 🔒
Watch the roster and re-negotiate the encode codec as peers join/leave, writing the decision into adapt.target_codec for the encode loop to act on. encode_caps is probed once by the caller (the probe isn’t free) and reused here. Lives for the publish — ends when view_rx closes (session torn down).
spawn_feedback_listener 🔒
Spawn the producer side of the receiver→producer BWE loop: watch the feedback_tx broadcast and, when a report names OUR video track of source, fold its loss into AdaptShared (max-reduced across reporters this interval — serve the worst leg). The adaptation tick drains it into the sender-side rate controller. Reports for other tracks (or a stale id) are ignored. “Our track” is resolved exactly like the keyframe listener.
spawn_keyframe_listener 🔒
Spawn the producer side of the PLI relay: watch the keyframe_tx broadcast and, when the requested TrackId is OUR video track of source (Camera vs Screen), mark a PLI pending in AdaptShared. The adaptation tick drains it through the throttle, coalescing a storm into a single IDR. “Our track” is resolved from the SessionView (a video track whose peer == self AND source == source).
