Module simulcast 

Source
Expand description

Demand-driven simulcast, producer side: LayerControl folds every receiver’s standing quality demand (QualityReport via spawn_quality_listener) into a hound_media::SimulcastPlanner, driving the shared LOW-layer supervisor; spawn_low_video_publish drains the capture thread’s low encode onto its own moq track, announcing it on the FIRST frame only (dark until actually producing). Invariant: the source encode is never dropped — the low layer is purely additive, so any low-layer fault costs a thumbnail, never the full-quality source.

Structs§

LayerControl 🔒
The per-source producer seam between the quality listener (records each receiver’s demand into a [SimulcastPlanner]) and the low-track publish supervisor (spawns / tears the low encoder). Mirrors AdaptShared as a shared producer control block; the planner is touched ~1 Hz (per report / roster change), never per frame, so the Mutex is free.

Constants§

SIMULCAST_ACTUATE 🔒
Gate for demand-driven simulcast. While false, [SimulcastPlanner] clamps to SOURCE_ONLY (the low layer is never spun up) — the demand pipeline still runs and is observable, but the publish path is byte-identical to a no-simulcast build.

Functions§

spawn_low_video_publish 🔒
Publish a LOW simulcast layer: drain the capture thread’s downscaled encode and forward it on its own moq track. The control-plane Publish announce is sent on the FIRST frame only, so the low track stays invisible to receivers until it’s actually producing (dark while no one demands it). No own BWE tick — the low layer’s bitrate is the planner’s cap, applied by the capture thread’s low_adapt.
spawn_quality_listener 🔒
Spawn the producer side of demand-driven simulcast: watch quality and, when a request names OUR video track of source, record it into layers (driving the low-layer supervisor). A slow tick drops requesters who left the call. “Our track” is resolved exactly like the feedback/keyframe listeners.
