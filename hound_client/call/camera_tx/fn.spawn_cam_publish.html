Spawn the camera publish task. When enabled, a blocking thread opens the engine Camera, pushes each frame’s I420 to the self-preview sink ("self"), H.264-encodes it, and queues the access units for the async publish half. When disabled, capture stops (the device is released). Self-preview works even if the publish/subscribe never gets a remote peer.

Sender-side BWE adaptation: a 1 s tick folds rtt_conn’s RTT + the worst receiver-reported loss (from the per-track feedback listener) into a target the capture/encode loop applies (bitrate retarget, fps gate, downscale to the rung, AudioOnly pause), and the keyframe listener arms forced IDRs on PLI. The adaptation is per-establish — rebuilt fresh on each reconnect (correct: the rate controller starts clean against the new connection).
