The broadcast-namespace convention every announce/subscribe must obey.

space/{space_id}/peer/{user_id}/{peer_id}/{track}

user_id is the AUTHENTICATED identity from the relay token / PeerInfo roster (a UUID) — the segment the relay enforces. peer_id is the control-plane-assigned u32 tail that keeps a single user’s multiple devices/sessions distinct within their own namespace.

The relay ALWAYS scopes each authenticated connection’s SERVE (subscribe) half to the space/{space_id} prefix taken from its relay token — moq-net refuses any subscribe outside it (no cross-space leakage). The INGEST (publish) half is ALWAYS additionally tightened to space/{space_id}/peer/{user_id}, so a connection can only announce under its own authenticated user’s sub-path — see handle_connection and peer_scope. Per-track allow-listing (e.g. gating who may publish a specific hound-control TrackId) remains a later refinement.
