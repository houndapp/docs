§hound-sfu

The Selective Forwarding Unit (SFU) for Hound voice channels.

§What is an SFU?

In a voice/video call every participant sends their audio and video to everyone else. Without an intermediary, participant A would need to upload their stream N-1 times (once per other participant). That multiplies upload bandwidth linearly and is impractical beyond 2-3 people.

An SFU sits in the middle: each client uploads once to the SFU, and the SFU forwards each stream to all subscribers. Upload stays constant regardless of how many people join.

§Transport

We use iroh, a QUIC-based P2P library. Clients connect to the SFU’s iroh node ID over QUIC. Within that single connection:

  • QUIC datagrams carry delta video frames and audio — unreliable delivery is fine here because a dropped frame is just a momentary quality blip, and real-time media can’t afford head-of-line blocking.
  • QUIC streams carry keyframes — these must arrive because a video decoder cannot start without them.

§Module layout

wire/   — byte-level encoding: frame headers, fragmentation, codec negotiation
room/   — pure state machine: who is in the room, how frames are forwarded
auth/   — relay token verification (HMAC-SHA256)
sfu/    — glue: iroh connections → room state machine

Each module is designed to be testable on its own without network I/O (the “sans-IO” pattern). See room::state for an example with FrameSink.
