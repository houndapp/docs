1//! # hound-media
2//!
3//! Shared, **sans-IO** media layer for Hound voice/video: the on-the-wire packet
4//! format and the pure frame packetization / reassembly logic, with no network,
5//! codec, async, or clock dependencies.
6//!
7//! ## Why
8//!
9//! The client and the SFU (and previously a TypeScript worker) each had their
10//! own copy of the framing logic, and they drifted — which is what broke video.
11//! This crate is the single source of truth for the wire contract, so the
12//! pieces cannot disagree, and every byte is covered by golden + round-trip
13//! tests that fail loudly if the format changes.
14//!
15//! ## Layers
16//!
17//! * [`packet`] — the fixed-size [`packet::PacketHeader`] and its enums
18//!   ([`packet::TrackKind`], [`packet::Codec`]), with byte-exact encode/decode.
19//! * [`fragment`] — [`fragment::Packetizer`] (split an encoded frame into
20//!   MTU-sized packets) and [`fragment::Reassembler`] (collect a frame's
21//!   fragments back, tolerating reorder, duplicates, and loss).
22//! * [`clock`] — [`clock::CaptureClock`], one monotonic timestamp source shared
23//!   by audio and video capture so their timestamps share a timeline (A/V sync).
24//! * [`jitter`] — [`jitter::PlayoutBuffer`], releases frames in timestamp order
25//!   on a playout schedule (absorbs jitter/reorder; not gated on audio).
26//! * [`recovery`] — [`recovery::NackGenerator`] / [`recovery::RetransmitBuffer`],
27//!   NACK-based loss recovery (repair lost packets without waiting for a keyframe).
28//! * [`congestion`] — [`congestion::RateController`] (loss-based bandwidth
29//!   estimate) + [`congestion::QualityLadder`] (adaptive video rung selection).
30//! * [`sim`] — a deterministic network-impairment simulator (loss/jitter/
31//!   reorder/duplication/bandwidth) for testing the engine without sockets.
32//!
33//! Transport (iroh), congestion control, jitter buffering, and codecs live
34//! elsewhere and are pumped with the types defined here.
35
