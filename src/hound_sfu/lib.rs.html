1//! # hound-sfu
2//!
3//! The Selective Forwarding Unit (SFU) for Hound voice channels.
4//!
5//! ## What is an SFU?
6//!
7//! In a voice/video call every participant sends their audio and video to
8//! *everyone else*. Without an intermediary, participant A would need to upload
9//! their stream N-1 times (once per other participant). That multiplies upload
10//! bandwidth linearly and is impractical beyond 2-3 people.
11//!
12//! An SFU sits in the middle: each client uploads **once** to the SFU, and
13//! the SFU forwards each stream to all subscribers. Upload stays constant
14//! regardless of how many people join.
15//!
16//! ## Transport
17//!
18//! We use [iroh](https://docs.rs/iroh), a QUIC-based P2P library. Clients
19//! connect to the SFU's iroh node ID over QUIC and speak the MoQ protocol
20//! (ALPN `moql`) on that connection. [`moq_relay`] forwards every publisher's
21//! encoded media to the other subscribers in the same space — moq-net's
22//! group/object model (one QUIC uni-stream per GOP, stale-group priority-drop)
23//! gives us keyframe-reliable / delta-unreliable delivery without hand-rolled
24//! framing or NACK caches.
25//!
26//! ## Module layout
27//!
28//! ```text
29//! auth/      — relay token verification (HMAC-SHA256)
30//! moq_relay/ — the MoQ forward plane: auth gate + namespace scoping over moq-net
31//! sfu/       — glue: binds the iroh endpoint and drives the accept loop
32//! ```
33

35pub mod moq_relay;
36pub mod sfu;
