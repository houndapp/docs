§Per-slot playout starvation classifier (pure, sans-IO)

The mix bus pulls each peer’s jitter buffer once per 10 ms tick. When a pull comes up short the bus must decide: is this an audible underflow (real voice stutter — the “audio is sacred” violation we must surface and alarm on) or expected silence (the peer is quiet, so Opus stopped sending / sends sparse comfort-noise frames — a dry carry that produces no audible gap)?

Counting silence as stutter is not cosmetic: it makes the metric cry wolf (the live reports showed a permanently-silent peer logging ~5 “starves” on every DTX gap, drowning out real glitches) and makes the invariant un-measurable. This module is the single discriminator, extracted from the bus so the policy is unit-tested in isolation against scripted tick sequences.

§The discriminator: recently-released energy

A dry carry is only audible if real audio was playing just before it. So the tracker watches the energy of the audio the slot releases: a peak above SILENCE_FLOOR marks the slot active; quiet output (comfort noise / the tail of speech) lets it decay back to silent after ACTIVE_DECAY_TICKS. A short carry while active counts; while silent it does not. This is the right rule regardless of why the carry is dry (DTX, a lost run of silence, the peer left): the test is “would a listener hear a gap”, and the answer is no when the recent signal was silence.

A genuinely abrupt stop (loud frame → immediate silence) still counts the first STARVE_IDLE_TICKS of the gap — the transient when someone stops mid-word — then falls silent. That is a real, brief, audible edge and counting it once is correct; the pathology this fixes is counting every gap of a continuously silent peer.

Constants§

ACTIVE_DECAY_TICKS
Ticks of quiet output after which a slot is considered silent (no recent real audio), so a dry carry reads as DTX rather than stutter. 8 ticks ≈ 80 ms at the 10 ms mix cadence. Must be ≥ STARVE_IDLE_TICKS so a real mid-speech gap is still counted for its full onset window before the slot is deemed silent.
SILENCE_FLOOR
Peak |sample| (i16 scale, 0..=32767) below which released audio is treated as silence / comfort noise rather than speech. ~328 ≈ 1% of full scale ≈ −40 dBFS: well above comfort-noise / dither, well below any intelligible speech peak.
STARVE_IDLE_TICKS
Empty-streak length past which even an active-looking gap stops counting: a peer that has been dry this long has stopped, not stuttered. 5 ticks ≈ 50 ms — long enough to register a real mid-speech dropout, short enough not to climb forever.
