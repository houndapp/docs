Expand description

Scale a planar I420 frame from src_w×src_h to dst_w×dst_h, resizing each plane independently: Y at full resolution and U/V at half resolution (the I420 chroma subsampling). This is the I420-native analogue of scale_rgb — used by the AVFoundation capturer (Apple) and the V4L2 capturer (Linux), which both produce I420 at the camera’s native resolution and must hand the encoder the requested encode geometry (the same contract the nokhwa path meets by scaling in RGB).

All dimensions must be even (I420 chroma is half-res). Returns None on a malformed input (zero/odd dimension or a short source buffer) so the caller skips the frame rather than feeding the encoder corrupt planes. The caller should short-circuit the equal-dimension case (no scale needed) before calling, since this always allocates and copies.

Live callers: the AVFoundation camera + SCK screen (Apple), the V4L2 camera (Linux), and the portal/PipeWire screen backend (Linux).
