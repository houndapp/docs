§Loss recovery: NACK generation + retransmission

Sans-IO building blocks for repairing packet loss without waiting for the next keyframe. These back the datagram/audio path and the impairment simulator; on the MoQ video path, QUIC’s own per-GOP-stream retransmit plays this role (see crate::fragment / hound-transport):

  • NackGenerator (receiver) watches the per-stream packet_seq sequence, detects gaps, and decides which sequence numbers to NACK (negative acknowledge) — with a retry cadence, a retry cap, and an age cutoff after which it gives up (and the decoder must recover at a keyframe instead).
  • RetransmitBuffer (sender) keeps the most recent packets so it can serve a retransmit when a NACK arrives.

Both are pure and clock-driven by the caller, so they are deterministic and testable against crate::sim. One NackGenerator and one RetransmitBuffer exist per (sender, track) sequence space. Sequence numbers are 32-bit and compared with wrapping arithmetic.
