§Connection-level coordinated send budget

Sans-IO pieces for the shared-uplink problem the MoQ multiplex creates: all of a client’s video tracks (camera + screen share) ride ONE QUIC connection, but each track runs its own per-track sender bitrate controller that sees only its receivers’ loss/RTT — never the connection’s own uplink saturation. Under a heavy screen share the sum of the per-track targets oversubscribes the wire, the shared publish channels drop, bufferbloat builds, and the client’s OWN downlink receive starves. This module is the missing coordinator’s pure core:

  • SendBudget — an AIMD estimate of the connection’s achievable TOTAL send rate, driven by aggregate publish-drop backpressure (the one connection-level congestion signal a sender can see without new transport surface). It decreases multiplicatively when the connection dropped publish AUs this interval and increases additively when it didn’t, converging toward the wire’s capacity.
  • allocate_send_budget — a max-min-fair split of a video budget across the active video tracks by their desired (uncapped) rates, with a per-track floor so a greedy screen share can’t starve the camera below a usable rate.

Both are deterministic: time enters only as an explicit now_us argument and there is no I/O, clock read, or hidden state — so the convergence and the allocation are fully unit-testable. The IO shell (the session coordinator) feeds the drop signal in and pushes the resulting caps onto each track’s adaptation seam; nothing here touches a socket or a thread.

Structs§

SendBudget
AIMD estimate of the connection’s achievable TOTAL send rate (bits/sec), driven by aggregate publish-drop backpressure. One per shared connection; the coordinator calls observe once per control interval (~1 Hz) with whether the connection over-produced (dropped publish AUs) this interval, and feeds the returned total into allocate_send_budget.

Constants§

DECREASE_COOLDOWN_US 🔒
Minimum spacing between multiplicative decreases. One isolated drop (a transient scheduling hiccup, a single late AU) must NOT collapse the budget; only sustained over-capacity — a drop in successive intervals past this cooldown — walks it down (≈ ×0.85 per second toward capacity). Increases are not cooled down (they are already gentle and clamped).
DECREASE_FACTOR 🔒
Multiplicative decrease factor applied to the total budget when the connection dropped publish AUs this interval. 0.85 ≈ a 15% back-off per congested second, matching the well-known AIMD decrease band: gentle enough that one stray drop (gated by DECREASE_COOLDOWN_US) doesn’t collapse the estimate, fast enough that sustained over-capacity converges toward the wire in a handful of seconds.
INCREASE_BPS 🔒
Additive increase per clean (no-drop) control interval. 500 kbps/s is a steady probe upward that recovers the full budget within a few seconds after the congestion clears, without overshooting hard enough to immediately re-congest.
MIN_VIDEO_BPS
The per-track floor: an active video track is never capped below this, so a greedy screen share can’t starve the camera to a black/frozen tile. 200 kbps is ≈ the lowest usable video rung (180p15-class); below it, video isn’t worth sending and the track’s own adaptation should have paused to AudioOnly.
