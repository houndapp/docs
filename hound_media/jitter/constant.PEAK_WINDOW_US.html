Expand description

How long an observed transit-deviation PEAK keeps the recommended depth provisioned, on the arrival clock. The smoothed mean J forgets a rare spike in under a second, but the spikes that actually pop audio are RECURRING tail events — a ~100 ms connection stall every 30–60 s (a video IDR/delta burst bufferbloating the uplink delays every stream at once; stream priority cannot reorder a downstream router’s queue). If the buffer relaxed between events, every recurrence would pop again; holding the peak for 90 s covers the observed recurrence interval, while a link that stays clean for 90 s relaxes back to floor + 4·J.
