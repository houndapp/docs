§Keyframe requests (PLI) and keyframe-generation throttling

Sans-IO building blocks for the “ask for a fresh keyframe” recovery path — the complement to NACK (crate::recovery). When loss can’t be repaired by retransmission (the NACK generator gave up, a frame failed to reassemble, or a receiver joined mid-stream), the decoder is stuck until the next keyframe. The receiver asks the producer for one with a Picture Loss Indication (PLI); the SFU relays it; the producer decides whether to force a keyframe.

  • KeyframeRequester (receiver) — tracks whether we are waiting for a decodable keyframe and rate-limits PLIs, so one in-flight request (RTT + encode time) does not trigger a storm before the keyframe can arrive.
  • KeyframeThrottle (producer) — coalesces a burst of PLIs (e.g. every receiver after one shared loss) into a single keyframe and enforces a minimum spacing between forced keyframes so the bitrate does not spike.

Both are pure and clock-driven by the caller, so they are deterministic and testable. One KeyframeRequester exists per (sender, track) we receive; one KeyframeThrottle per track we produce.
