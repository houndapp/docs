Shared group/object drain used by MoqSubscriber and MoqRelaySubscriber — the only difference between the two is how the TrackConsumer was obtained (direct Server::accept vs. Client::connect to the relay); the priority-drop drain discipline is identical.

Drains one group (GOP) at a time via [TrackConsumer::next_group] (priority-drop: a stale group is discarded at its boundary rather than head-of-line-blocking a newer keyframe); within a group, objects are read in order. Each object’s OBJ_HEADER_LEN-byte envelope is decoded back into a Frame and the encoded payload handed out verbatim.

§Known limitation — within-GOP head-of-line stall under heavy loss

next_group’s priority-drop only fires at a group boundary: it discards a stale group instead of head-of-line-blocking a newer keyframe, but only once the current group has drained. A subscriber already part-way through group K can still stall inside K if an early object of K is lost — QUIC retransmits it, but moq-net prioritises the newest streams, so under continuous production K’s retransmit can be starved behind newer GOPs and the inner GroupConsumer::read_frame blocks. Recovery is then bounded by QUIC eventually retransmitting K’s missing object, which under a sustained, contiguous downstream loss burst can be slow. Preemptively abandoning the stalled GOP to jump to the next keyframe does not reliably help: when the burst also drops the newer GOP’s opener, next_group has nothing newer to offer either. The fault-injection e2e (services/sfu/tests/moq_relay_faults.rs) characterises the recoverable regime; see its module docs.

reorder_ok = true drains via [TrackConsumer::recv_group] (ARRIVAL order, never discards) instead of [TrackConsumer::next_group] (monotonic, drops a group whose sequence ≤ one already returned). Set it for AUDIO: audio groups are small and frequent, and on the shared connection a high-bitrate screenshare reorders those tiny audio streams, so next_group’s staleness rule would silently drop reordered ones — fatal, since a slightly-reordered Opus frame is perfectly playable (the jitter buffer reorders by timestamp and the decoder skips truly-stale frames). Video keeps next_group: a stale GOP genuinely should be dropped for a newer keyframe.
